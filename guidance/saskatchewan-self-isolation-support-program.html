Saskatchewan - Self-Isolation Support Program

This advice was last updated on 04/29/20 at 18:26 PDT.

The Self-Isolation Support Program is targeted at Saskatchewan workers forced to self-isolate in order to curb the spread of COVID-19.

What it is

Adult workers (18+) who are not covered by recent federally announced employment insurance programs and other supports may be eligible for this program which will provide $450 per week, for a maximum of two weeks or $900.

Who is eligible

To be eligible, adult workers (18+) in Saskatchewan must be in self-isolation for one of the following reasons:

  • They have contracted COVID-19 or are showing symptoms.
  • They have been in contact with an individual infected with COVID-19.
  • They have recently returned from international travel and have been required to self-isolate.

Workers in self-isolation, or likely to be, due to one of the above reasons are eligible if:

  • They are not eligible for compensation, including sick leave or vacation leave from their employer.
  • They do not have private insurance.
  • They are not covered by other programs such as federal employment insurance that has been updated.
