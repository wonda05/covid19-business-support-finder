Work-Sharing Temporary Special Measures to Support Employers and Workers Affected by COVID-19

This advice was last updated on 04/07/20 at 23:35 PDT.

Special measures to support employers and workers in the work-sharing program.

What it is

Effective March 15, 2020 to March 14, 2021, the Government of Canada is introducing temporary special measures to:

  • Extend maximum possible duration of an agreement from 38 weeks to 76 weeks
  • Waive mandatory cooling off period
  • Reduce requirements and expand eligibility to
    • Include
      • Business who have been in business for only 1 year
      • Government Business Enterprises (GBEs) (Public Corporations)
      • Not-for-profit employers
    • Include core staff (year-round, permanent, full-time or part-time employees)
    • Include staff considered essential to the recovery and viability of the business

Who is eligible

You are an eligible:

  • If you are a:
    • Year-round business in Canada for at least 1 year
    • Private business or a publicly held company
    • Not for profit employer (experiencing a shortage of work due to a reduction of business activity and/or a reduction in revenue levels due to COVID-19)
    • Government Business Enterprises (GBEs), also referred to as public corporations
  • Have at least 2 employees in the work-sharing unit

How to access

To apply for the Work-Sharing program, employers must submit:

Applications for a Work-Sharing Agreement form (EMP5100)

Attachment A: Work-Sharing Unit form (EMP5101)
