Self–employment Income Support Scheme

This advice was last updated on 02/04/20 at 15:46 GMT.

Advice on the scheme if you're self-employed or a member of a partnership and have lost income due to coronavirus.

What it is

Self employed people can claim a taxable grant worth 80% of average trading profits up to a maximum of £2,500 per month for the next 3 months. The scheme will be extended into further months if necessary.

Who is eligible

Either self employed individuals or partnerships who have: submitted Income Tax Self Assessment tax return for 2018-19, traded in the tax year 2019-20, are trading when you apply (or would be except for COVID-19), intend to continue trading in the tax year 2019-20, and have lost trading/partnership profits due to COVID-19.

Your trading profits must also be less than £50,000 and half of your income must come from self-employment.

How to access

You cannot apply for this scheme yet, HMRC will contact you if you are eligible for the scheme and invite you to apply online. The government hope to have the scheme operational by the beginning of June.
