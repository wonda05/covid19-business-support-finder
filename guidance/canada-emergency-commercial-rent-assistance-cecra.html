Canada Emergency Commercial Rent Assistance (CECRA)

This advice was last updated on 05/01/20 at 20:25 PDT.

The Government of Canada has reached an agreement with all provinces and territories to implement the Canada Emergency Commercial Rent Assistance (CECRA) for small businesses. This program will lower rent by 75% for small businesses that have been affected by COVID-19.

What it is

The program will provide forgivable loans to qualifying commercial property owners to cover three months of rent payments owed by their small business tenants who are experiencing financial hardship during April, May, and June. The loans will be forgiven if the mortgaged property owner agrees to reduce the small business tenants’ rent by at least 75% under a rent forgiveness agreement. The small business tenant would cover the remainder of the rent, up to 25%.

Who is eligible

You are eligible if:

  • You own property that generates rental revenue from commercial real property located in Canada.
  • You declared rental income on your tax return for 2018 and/or 2019.
  • You have impacted tenants who:
    • Pay no more than $50,000 monthly gross rent per location
    • Generate no more than $20 million in gross annual revenues
    • Have temporarily ceased operations or experienced at least 70% decline in pre-COVID-19 revenues
  • You have entered or will enter into a rent reduction agreement that reduces rent by at least 75% and includes a moratorium on eviction for April, May, and June 2020.
