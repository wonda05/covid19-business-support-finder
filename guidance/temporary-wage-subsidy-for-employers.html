10% Temporary Wage Subsidy for Employers

This advice was last updated on 04/07/20 at 21:20 PST.

The federal government has provided a new Temporary Wage Subsidy (TWS) to eligible employers for the period March 18, 2020 to June 19, 2020 as a result of the COVID-19 pandemic.

What it is

The 10% TWS is a federal government program that allows eligible employers to reduce the amount of their payroll remittances to the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) equal to 10% of the remuneration paid from March 18, 2020 to June 19, 2020, up to $1,375 for each eligible employee, and up to a maximum of $25,000 per employer.

Who is eligible

You are an eligible employer if you:

  • Are a(n):
    • individual (excluding trusts)
    • partnership
    • non-profit organization
    • registered charity
    • Canadian-controlled private corporation (including a cooperative corporation) eligible for the small business deduction
  • Have an existing business number and payroll program account with the CRA on March 18, 2020; and
  • Pay salary, wages, bonuses, or other remuneration to an individual employed in Canada, between March 18, 2020 and June 19, 2020.

How to access

No application needed. Eligible employers can start reducing the remittances of income tax withheld from employees and sent to CRA by the amount of the subsidy on the first remittance that includes remuneration paid between March 18, 2020 and June 19, 2020. Eligible employers must keep the information that supports their subsidy calculation.
